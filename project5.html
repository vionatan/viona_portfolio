Icon and App Design: ARcMotion

Overview

ARcmotion is an app meant for people who needs physio-therapy especially for out-patients. In this app, it incorporates game-style exercises to motivate and distract users from their focus on putting out their energy to perform an exercise, preventing them from giving up mid-way. Subsequently, there are interactive videos exercise to guide them to better understanding of a certain exercise and helps to measure the angle of position.

Competitor research

Doing therapy regularly improves a persons abilities, so changes towards a therapy exercise are frequent. Instead of attending scheduled appointment dates to receive the updated medical instruction from their therapist, physio therapy apps had advanced to allow video calls and texting with their therapist and get instructions directly from their therapist without wasting any time. Icon design for ARcMotion

Description:
The icon comprises of a lady in a yoga position, holding onto a rope that comes from the waves and swinging towards the waves to symbolise active exercise. As she swings, she starts to disintegrate and seems to be integrating with the incoming waves. Therefore it symbolises if users continues to carry out their exercises regularly, they will become flexible and strong just like the avatar in the icon.

Wireframe design

These are the key wireframes for the ARcmotion app. ARcmotion uses AR to capture your movements and count the number of times you move. For example, capturing how many times you Squat. I wanted the app to adopt mini games which will make therapy more enjoyable and game-style like, which will motivate and distract users from focusing on fatigue. users can unlock new achievements when they reach a certain goal.
