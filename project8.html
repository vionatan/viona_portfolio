Design a bulk ordering website

Delightful Bakery is a neighborhood bakery that offers a wide variety of freshly baked goods and customisable cake service. The bulking ordering website is created to help customers to have an easy ordering process and to keep track of their orders.

The problem and goal

Frequent customers of Delightful Bakery have stressful moments while performing bulk orders. Designing a bulk ordering website such that users can have easy access to ordering and coordinating their orders.

User research

User Interview

Prior to the user interview, I prepare a few questions that are linked to the problem and goal statement:

  1. How often do you frequent the bakery?
  2. What do you dislike about ordering from this bakery and how does it make you feel?
  3. Usually on what occasions will you order in bulk?
  4. Why did you choose to buy from the bakery?
  5. What are some of the challenges you face while ordering in bulk?

I sent these questions into an AI to help me generate 5 user interview scripts and subsequently group them based on similar answers and identify their pain points.

One key user segment that emerged from the research comprises frequent customers of delightful bakery who struggles to coordinate pick-ups and delivery for their orders. Various challenges such as picking an available date and time from their schedule, traffic considerations and the bakery's ability to complete the order prevents users from coordinating the pick-ups and delivery at ease.

Pain Points

With the given factors contributing to the research problem, I have gathered a few pain points from the interview script:


Accessibility to baked goods


certain baked products are sold out quickly when they arrive at the bakery

Plan time and date constraints


When ordering in bulk, they have to consider traffic conditions, date and time of the occasion, making coordination difficult

Slow cake customisation service


From discussing the design with the bakery to deciding on the date can be very time consuming

To start off my design, I draft out multiple iterations of paper wireframes for each screen. Then based on the persona,sitemap and drawing inspriration from our competitors. These would be the first version of the Delightful bakery website's wireframes, adopting the z-layout to present the content, for example, the homepage allow users to scan through important information regarding the bakery easily. Usability Testing and Finding

With the low-fidelity prototype, I decided to conduct the first usability testing to gather some feedbacks from my friends and family to improve the user experience and needs. The prototype showcases the main flow of the app which is purchasing a craft supply.

These are the tasks and question that the users had to complete during the testing:

1 Find a way to view the products page
2 add a product to cart after customising
3 View your cart and proceed to the checkout
4 Complete the checkout process and confirm order
5 How do you find the checkout process?
6 Check the schedules for your upcoming orders

After the user testing, I retrieved all the answers from the interview notes and subsequently categorising them based on the app's features and grouping together those that exhibited similar responses.Here are my finding to help improve the crafTy app:

1 Users prefer fulfilment date calendar selector instead of typing it
2 “select the appropriate time of delivery” is confusing for users to understand
3 For the my orders page, the upcoming orders is not obvious enough

Key Takeaways

1

This case study project has enlightened me on the importance of ensuring website responsiveness across various devices. Having to meticulously adjust font sizes, optimise image and text placement, and maintaining an organized layout to facilitate clear and uncluttered readability for users.

2

I learned that it is important to take a step back and look at the wireframe designs from a user perspective helps me to identify weak areas of my design.
